I consider teaching to be a great privilege and always enjoy the opportunity to interact with students. My teaching philosophy centers on 1) experimenting with ways to enhance the pedagogical experience and 2) making every effort to get students as interested in the material as I am. I have consistently been ranked in the top percentiles of student evaluations at the department, college, and University levels. For a copy of my evaluations, please contact me. In 2020, I was recognized by the College of Behavioral and Social Science with their Excellence in Teaching Award. I have served as a teaching assistant for the following courses:

  • GVPT 202: Politics, Constitutional Policy, and the Institution of the U.S. Supreme Court (Patrick Wohlfarth, Primary Instructor), Spring 2019.
  • GVPT 473: The U.S. Congress (Ric Uslaner, Primary Instructor), Fall 2018.
In the Summer 2021 semester I was the instructor of record for:

GVPT 379I: Interest Group and External Influence in the 2020 US Presidential Election.
